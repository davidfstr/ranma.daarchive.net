A simple statement. That was all it took for Katsuhito's secret to be revealed. He could not fault Ranma's mother for it. No, even though she could somehow see through his disguise field, she was still unaware of his reasons behind keeping his true appearance hidden. The reasons he left Jurai in the first place. The reason he did not marry Ayeka, his half sister. And so, with the quiet sigh of a man who knew he was doomed, he turned to Ayeka and began to finally tell her the truth.

"Ayeka, I'm afraid I haven't been totally honest with you. You see, I'm not as old as you perceive me to be." said Katsuhito

Smirking, Washu began planning to get Ranma's mother into her lab sometime soon. She knew about Katsuhito's disguise field but had decided not to mention it earlier. For some reason Ranma's mother could see through a Juraian disguise field, something even she couldn't do with all her high tech toys. All she was able to accomplish was the detection of one in operation. Oh yes, she would be studying Ranma's mother in great detail sometime in the near future.

Frowning, Ayeka replied, "Oh? What do you mean?"

Sighing, Katsuhito asked, "Are you familiar with a Juraian disguise field?"

Shaking her head, Ayeka replied, "No. I've heard of them in passing but never sought to learn more. From what I know, only covert agents of the empire use them. Why do you ask?"

Closing his eyes, Katsuhito deactivated his disguise field. Several gasps came from the spectators around the table. Most likely from the newer arrivals he thought. He knew that he'd get those kind of reactions. What else would one do if they saw an old man suddenly become young. Finally, ready to face the music, he opened his eyes to see the facial reactions of those watching.

To his surprise, the newer arrivals barely blinked an eye. He was a bit curious as to how they could accept something so easily. Then again from what he'd heard of Ranma's life, they probably had to learn to adapt to the unusual very quickly.

"Grandpa?" asked Tenchi, obviously confused.

"Yes it is me." said Katsuhito.

"But how?" asked Tenchi.

"Yes brother. Please tell us why you lied about your true appearance and not returning to Jurai." said Ayeka, her voice as cold as a frigidaire.

Gulping at the glare his own sister was focusing at him, Katsuhito sat down and said, "I did not lie about everything. My ship was destroyed when I was battling Ryoko. I had no way to return to Jurai nor any way to communicate either. For all intents and purposes, I was marooned here."

"Grandpa." said Tenchi, indicating the new arrivals.

Seeing Tenchi's concern, Katsuhito said, "Do not worry about it Tenchi. They will be here for a while and will learn of this eventually if they do not know about it already."

"Yeah, don't worry about us sugar. This is nothing compared to some of the stuff we've seen. Right Kasumi?" said Ukyo.

"Oh my, yes. Ranma has so many interesting friends that come and visit." replied Kasumi.

"Err, right." said Ukyo while Ryoko just looked at Kasumi strangely.

"Now girls, let them continue. This seems to be very important." said Nodoka.

"Yes Auntie." they replied in tandem.

While Nodoka took a seat next to Katsuhito, Ayeka asked, "Why didn't you use your tree to call for help?"

"My tree was weak from the battle. She barely had enough strength to survive let alone provide me with energy. So in order to save her, I did the only thing I could do. I planted her into the soil so she could take root and survive." replied Katsuhito.

Her anger easing slightly, Ayeka asked, "If that is the case, then how can you still be this young?"

"Something unexpected happened. I only found out about it some ten years after I had built the shrine so that I could keep an eye on Ryoko's tomb. I noticed that my aging was still as slow as ever. And it was slowing with each passing year. With my tree having taken root, I should have started aging more rapidly. Similar to how the people of earth age. When I looked into it, I found that somehow, Ryoko's gems were supplying my tree with power. In turn, this was keeping me from aging as fast as I should have been." replied Katsuhito.

Confused, Ayeka asked, "But how is that possible? Only the water of life and the Power of Jurai can keep a Juraian from aging."

"I'll field this one Katsuhito." piped up Washu before continuing, "You see Ayeka, for some reason, the gems that give Ryoko her power are very similar to the Power of Jurai. How do you think Ryoko was able to get through Jurai's defenses so easily?"

"You mean?" asked Ayeka.

Nodding, Washu replied, "Yes, the energy of the gems comes from a source that is similar to Tsunami. As long as there is a single gem in the hilt of the master key, Katsuhito will age very slowly. But I do not know much more than that at the moment."

"I didn't mean to hurt you Ayeka. But you know as well as I do that we would not have had any peace on Jurai. Back then, prejudices of other races ran deep among the houses. Father was one of the first to see beyond that narrow minded view." said Katsuhito.

Sighing, Ayeka said, "Why didn't you tell me earlier? When we first learned you were indeed my brother."

"I did not wish to hurt you. I thought it would be better if you believed that I was too old for you to pursue. Tell me, would it have made any difference if I had revealed my true age and appearance to you then? Would you have tried to pursue me?" said Katsuhito.

"I... I don't know. Perhaps. But I still would have wanted to know the truth and not have been lied to." replied Ayeka.

As Katsuhito and Ayeka had their heart to heart talk, the others around the table watched while keeping silent. To the residents of the shrine, this single conversation would dictate how these two would interact with one another from that point on. To Ukyo, Kasumi, and Nodoka, the conversation had little meaning to them. However each listened in order to learn more of this obviously alien race.

*Oh my, this is so sad. He left her so that they would both have peace in their lives. It sounds like it's right out of a romantic manga.* thought Kasumi.

*Wow, this race that Ranchan is part of sounds complex. They even allow brothers and sisters to marry.* thought Ukyo.

*To put the well being of his sister before himself. He's so Manly!* thought Nodoka.

"I'm sorry Ayeka. I should have told you right away. But at the time I thought it to be for the best." said Katsuhito.

"Best! How is it for the best that I don't know the truth! I would think you'd trust me more than this!" half yelled Ayeka.

Hanging his head, Katsuhito replied, "I'm sorry. It was wrong of me and yes, I should have trusted you enough to tell you the truth."

Calming down slightly, Ayeka asked, "Do mother and father know?"

Sighing, Katsuhito said, "Funaho knows. I don't know about Misaki. As for father, I don't know one way or the other."

At the mention of Misaki, Happosai stiffened momentarily. However, as soon as Cologne made her presence known again, he relaxed.

Gazing at Katsuhito critically for a few moments, Ayeka finally sighed and asked, "How am I supposed to trust you again? You lied to me. Your own sister."

"As I said before, I am sorry, and I will try and find someway to make it up to you." said Katsuhito.

Thinking a moment, Ayeka began to develop a devilish little smirk. "Oh brother dear?" she said.

Gulping at the smirk that reminded him so much of Misaki, Katsuhito said, "Yes?"

"I might find it in my heart to forgive you if you do just one little thing for me." said Ayeka.

"What would that be?" asked Katsuhito.

A sudden chill went down Tenchi's spine as he got a slight premonition of something big happening soon. He'd gotten that same chill the day his mother died, the day he found Ryoko, the day he fought Kagato, the day he went after Clay, and the day he met his great grandparents. Silently he prayed that he would still be alive after whatever was about to happen took place.

"I will forgive you and release you from our betrothal if you will give me your permission and blessings to... " said Ayeka.

Everyone leaned forward to hear the next words that would leave Ayeka's mouth.

"...wed Tenchi." said Ayeka.

Katsuhito waited for the inevitable explosion of Ryoko who would begin arguing with Ayeka over Tenchi. However, when none came, he cracked open an eye to see why she was oddly silent. What greeted his vision was very odd to say the least. Ryoko was sitting there, sipping tea, and smiling.

Washu too thought something was odd. Usually Ryoko would get very violent and attack Ayeka when something like this came up. But for some reason, Ryoko was just sitting there quietly sipping tea. Curious, Washu decided to use their link to find out what was going on.

[Why are you being quiet Ryoko?] asked Washu mentally.

A moment passed before Ryoko responded, [It's just something Ayeka said. It reminded me of something that I had mostly overlooked until now.]

[More like forgot.] said Washu mentally.

[Hey!] responded Ryoko through the link.

[So what was it?] asked Washu again, genuinely curious.

[Oh nothing much. It's just that since Tenchi is the crown prince and will become emperor one day, he will have to eventually marry. When he does, it is Juraian custom for the emperor to have at least two wives.] said Ryoko, showing an unusual amount of intelligence for once.

[You're acting unusually smart. How did this come about?] asked Washu.

[Ever since I joined with Zero, I've been doing alot of thinking about things. How I felt about things and people. I've also been studying up on Juraian law and customs.] responded Ryoko.

[Ah, I see now. You're not only in love with Tenchi. You're also in love with Ayeka as well.] said Washu.

[What! No way do I care about that old hag!] retorted Ryoko.

[Come now Ryoko. Who do you think you're kidding. You're in love with the both of them and you know it.] said Washu.

[I... I... Alright fine! I am in love with both of them! Ya happy now?] responded Ryoko.

[I just wanted to make sure that you acknowledged it yourself. And, that I will help you in whatever way I can. I meant what I said the other night Ryoko. You will make a wonderful mother one day. Perhaps that day won't be too far into the future.] said Washu.

[Um...Thank You... Mom.] replied Ryoko with real emotion behind the word mom.

[You're welcome dear.] responded Washu.

The conversation between Washu and Ryoko only took a few moments. During those moments, the others around the table were watching intently as Ryoko continued to sit in peace and not utter a single response to Ayeka's proposal.

Tenchi wasn't paying much attention though. He was still praying that Ayeka and Ryoko would not hurt anyone when the fighting began. He didn't want Ranma's mother and friends to be hurt by one of Ayeka and Ryoko's fights.

Washu tossed Katsuhito a look that told him to add a little something to Ayeka's proposal or suffer one of her many experiments.

Gulping at the look Washu gave him, Katsuhito thought about Ayeka's proposal for a moment. *Well, it is what I have been trying to do for some time now. And Ayeka did say she'd forgive me if I consented. But can I do that to Tenchi without his approval? Hmm... Then there's Ryoko's odd behavior. Could she have finally figured out that Tenchi is supposed to have two wives by Jurian custom? It is possible. Perhaps if I... Yes, that might just work.* thought Katsuhito.

While Katsuhito thought, Cologne mearly smiled. *This Ayeka may not be a fighter, but she does have considerable power if what I saw last night was any indication. Then again it didn't affect Ranma very much. Perhaps due to the boy's origins. But this Ayeka does have a keen mind as well. I wonder if I should try and start another Amazon society here in Japan. There certainly isn't any shortage of strong blood. Hmm... I will have to give this more thought later. For now, I need to plan for tonight. You're in for quite a surprise tonight Happi. I mean to make up for those three hundred years we were apart. With interest.* thought Cologne.

Happosai shivered as though someone walked over his grave.

Clearing his throat to get everyone's attention, Katsuhito turned to Ayeka. "Ayeka, I shall give you my permission and blessings to wed Tenchi." he said.

Smiling, Ayeka replied, "Thank you very much brother. I forgive you and release you from our betrothal. I know that Tenchi and I shall be very happy together."

Before Ayeka could say more, Katsuhito interrupted her, "However..." he said.

"However?" asked Ayeka, her glare of doom beginning to form.

Trying to ignore Ayeka's forming glare, Katsuhito continued, "However, the final decision is Tenchi's. You may wed him, if that is what he desires."

"Very well. I shall adhere to Lord Tenchi's decision." said Ayeka, confident that Tenchi would indeed choose to marry her.

"Huh?" said Tenchi.

Smiling, Katsuhito said, "The decision is yours Tenchi. However, do not decide right away. I wish to talk to you in private before your decision is made about your first wife."

"Um... Ok... I think..." replied Tenchi, obviously confused about what was happening. From what he could figure, Ayeka had finally cornered him on making a decision. Ryoko had yet to make a violent retort. He couldn't figure out what his grandfather meant with that remark about his first wife. And Ranma's mother was giving him a very odd look.

Peaking out from the kitchen, Ranma surveyed the room. From his perspective, everything was peaceful. Ayeka was smiling. Ryoko was smiling. Tenchi was sweating buckets. Cologne was snuggled up with Happosai. Mousse looked as confused as ever. Kasumi was sniffling while wiping her eyes with a handkerchief. Ukyo was shaking her head slowly. Katsuhito seemed to have gotten younger. How that happened, he didn't know. All he could tell was it was the old man from his energy signature. And his mother was giving Tenchi a look that just screamed that Tenchi was very manly. He sweatdropped at that.

Returning to the kitchen, Ranma helped Sasami get everything ready to be served.

"Is everyone ok oneeniichan?" asked Sasami.

Scratching his head, Ranma replied, "I think so. Ayeka and Ryoko are smiling and Tenchi's nervous for some reason. I think something happened but I can't figure out what. My mom... well the less said about the look she's giving him, the better."

"That's unusual for Ayeka and Ryoko to be smiling for no reason. Tenchi-nichan is always nervous when Ayeka and Ryoko get along. I wonder what happened." said Sasami.

"I don't know. But whatever it was, it's made my mom thing that he's very manly." replied Ranma.

"What?" asked Sasami.

Sighing, Ranma said, "My mom's got some weird ideas about being a man. I'm not even sure I know all of them. All I know, is that somehow, she greatly approves of Tenchi for some reason."

"Um... What about you?" said Sasami.

Chuckling, Ranma said, "I'm not too sure. I know she thinks I'm manly and all that. But I also know she cares about me and that there are other parts to me than just being a man. Heh, I probably wouldn't put it past her to think I'm being manly even when I'm a girl. Which would be true in some sense since I only like girls even when I am one."

"She sounds weird. But my own mom has her quirks too." said Sasami.

"Oh? What kind?" asked Ranma.

Thinking a moment, Sasami replied, "Well she loves chocolate. She can't get enough of it. The same goes for anything sweet. Also, if she ever gets mad at you, call her a pretty young lady. She'll usually forgive you almost instantly."

"Yer right. She does have some quirks." said Ranma.

"True, but she's my mommy and I love her."

"Yeah, I love my mom as well." replied Ranma.

"And I love you too dear." said a woman's voice from right behind Ranma.

"Ack!" said a startled Ranma. Quickly turning around, he was met with the sight of his mother chuckling softly.

Still slightly chuckling, Nodoka said, "I'm sorry about that dear. I didn't mean to startle you."

Scratching the back of his head, Ranma replied, "Um... It's ok mom. Better you than someone else. So what brings you here?"

"I just thought you two might like some help with lunch." said Nodoka.

"That's alright mom. We were just finishing up actually." replied Ranma.

"Oh where are my manners. I haven't been properly introduced to this little one. Hello dear, my name is Nodoka. I'm Ranma's mother." said Nodoka to Sasami.

Smiling, Sasami replied, "A pleasure to meet you. I'm Sasami. I'm Ranma's little sister."

"Such a proper young lady. But how are you his little sister?" said Nodoka.

"That's kind of a long story mom. The short version is that Sasami hurt herself very badly when she was younger. My other mother, Tsunami, appeared and merged with Sasami to save her life. When Sasami gets older, she and Tsunami will merge into one being. Until then, Sasami is going to be my little sister." said Ranma.

Blinking a moment at the new information, Nodoka replied, "I see. Well I must say that she is very beautiful and sweet. But where are her parents?"

"They're on Jurai. I'm staying here with my sister and everyone else." said Sasami while blushing a little from the praise her oneeniichan's mother gave her.

Smiling, Nodoka said, "Well in that case, since Ranma's adopted you as his little sister, how would you like it if I adopted you as my daughter while your parents are away?"

Excited that she could have a mother while her real parents were away, Sasami said "Really?"

Nodding, Nodoka smiled and said, "Yes, really."

Crying out in delight, Sasami rushed forward and gave Nodoka a big hug. Ranma mearly hung back and smiled at the scene. He knew his mom always wanted a daughter. Well, now she had a son, as well as two daughters.

"Congratulations mom. It's a girl." said a chuckling Ranma.

Smiling, Nodoka said, "Why you, get over here and give your mother and sister a hug."

Spreading his arms, Ranma joined the group hug. It only lasted a few moments, but the love was very palatable. Once it was complete, the three separated.

"Well, now that we have that out of the way, is there anything a mother can do to help her two children." said a smiling Nodoka.

Smiling, Ranma said, "Well, we could use some help to take everything out to the table."

"All right dear." Nodoka said.

While lunch was about to be served at the Masaki household, two people on the other side of the universe finished their own lunch.

In the quarters of Ramsus, Tokimi's first chosen warrior, two people sat and enjoyed the nutrition that was required to sustain their bodies. Of course they could have gone without eating anything due to Tokimi's power. However it was something that both enjoyed doing.

"How was your meal?" asked Ramsus.

Thinking about it a moment, Nabiki replied, "It wasn't bad. Although I'm not really certain of what I just ate."

Chuckling a little, Ramsus said, "It's a dish that originated in a remote corner of the galaxy. I picked up the recipe while traveling through the area. Some of the ingredients are hard to come by."

"Oh? When was this?" asked Nabiki.

"It was before I came into the service of Lady Tokimi. At the time, I was just wandering the universe battling those I came across and trying to learn more about life." replied Ramsus.

Curious, Nabiki asked, "How did you end up here?"

Thinking back on it for a minute or two, Ramsus said, "Well I suppose it all started about a year ago. I was traveling through a section of the galaxy that was mostly unexplored. I was curious about what might be in that region. There, I met up with someone in the service of Lady Tokimi. For the life of me, I cannot remember their name. My memories of that time are scattered. All I can remember is an intense battle unlike any I've been in before. After that, my memory is a blank until I woke up here. Lady Tokimi saved my life and in turn, I swore to serve her."

Contemplating his story for a moment, Nabiki couldn't help but feel that it paralleled a certain pigtailed youth and some of his life. Looking up, she studied the man before her and compared him to the object of her revenge. Both were similar in that they loved to battle. However the man before her was more mature by light years. Not only in his personality and demeanor, but in appearance as well. Where Ranma was still a boy, the man before her was just that. A man. She didn't know how she felt about him and their new partnership, but for one of the first times in her life, she felt safe with someone. She didn't know why or whether it was some side effect of Lady Tokimi's power that coursed through them. All she knew is that for now, she had found an ally that might be able to help her finish what she started back on earth.

"How about you? What brought you here?" asked Ramsus, breaking Nabiki out of her contemplation.

Sadly, Nabiki said, "My younger sister was killed recently. Her fiancee had returned and told us how she died. But there were holes in his story. The biggest being that he killed a phoenix god."

Sucking in a breath, Ramsus said, "A phoenix? Are you sure?"

Looking at him a moment, Nabiki said, "What? You know something about it?"

Thinking a moment, Ramsus replied, "A phoenix is a very rare and powerful creature. Most races talk about them only in legends. However if one were on the planet you came from, then others would have noticed it. Their power is great enough that it is easily sensed. If nobody sensed it, then the phoenix god you were talking about was most likely either a being that had some phoenix ancestry or an avatar of a phoenix. But even then, a being like that would not be beaten by a mortal very easily."

"Interesting. You are probably right that it wasn't a true phoenix then. Anyways, as I was saying, my late sister's fiancee told us the story of what happened to her. While he told the story, I couldn't help but feel that there was something that wasn't right. I mean, how could a martial artist, no matter how good, kill a being that powerful?" said Nabiki.

"They couldn't. The only way would be to have more power." replied Ramsus.

"Exactly. I figured that he had enough power to defeat that thing all along. I only recently learned that one of his mothers is Tsunami, Lady Tokimi's sister. So instead of saving my sister, he was being his usual cocky self and holding back. Playing with that thing until my little sister was dead. Then he went and killed it. He let her die." said Nabiki.

"Very interesting, but that still does not answer my question." said Ramsus.

"I'm getting to that. You see, the next day he left for somewhere. I don't know where but that night I went to kill his mother. I was determined to make him pay for letting my sister die. However he stopped me just as I was swinging her sword toward her neck." said Nabiki.

"How did he stop you? If he was gone, he should not have been able to." said Ramsus.

"I don't know. All I know is that one moment I'm swinging her sword and the next, it's glowing and stuck in mid air. Then his voice comes from nowhere and wakes her up. She unmasked me and I ran from my house. After that, I was brought here. I agreed to serve Lady Tokimi so that I would have the power to kill him." said Nabiki.

"Hmm... That is an odd scenario. Tell me, was there anything odd about the sword?" asked Ramsus.

Thinking about it a moment, Nabiki nodded, "Yeah, now that you mention it, there was something odd about it. It wasn't the one she normally carried around. It was more elaborate and detailed. From what I could see in the dark, it had a relief etched into the blade and scabbard. A hawk in flight and a panther prowling."

His eyes widening, Ramsus said, "I think I may know how he stopped you. That sword must have been made by the Light Hawk Wings. However there is something else that worries me more. You said there was a relief of a panther on the sword and scabbard?"

"Yeah. What about it?" said Nabiki.

"Tell me, does this guy you're after know any techniques involving cats?" asked Ramsus, his voice deadly serious.

Gazing into his eyes, Nabiki said, "Yeah, just one I can think of. A technique his idiot father tried to teach him."

"What was the name of that technique?" asked Ramsus.

"The Neko-ken. Why? What's so important about it?" said Nabiki.

His gaze very serious, Ramsus replied, "Because, that technique was one developed by Lady Tokimi herself millennia ago to train cannon fodder as berserkers. It's a technique of death and destruction that allows those trained in it to tap into her power. How anyone on your planet knows of it, is a mystery. The only reason he probably didn't go permanently insane is because he is Tsunami's child. The only good thing I can think of is that he most likely doesn't have conscious control of it."

Wincing slightly, Nabiki said, "Actually, he does. I saw him using it the day I was going to kill his mother."

"We must inform Lady Tokimi of this immediately! The child of Tsunami must not be allowed to develop that power any further!" said Ramsus, his voice as hard as steel.

His words sinking in, Nabiki could only nod. To her, this was not good. It appeared Ranma had access to her lady's power.

Thinking for a moment, Washu smiled and said, "I'll tell you what Kasumi-chan. Instead of letting you learn on your own, I'll take you under my wing and teach you all there is to know about Health and Medicine. How does that sound?"

Smiling happily, Kasumi nodded, "Oh my, that would be wonderful! Thank you very much sensei!"

A frightening scene came to Ayeka's mind at the moment.
