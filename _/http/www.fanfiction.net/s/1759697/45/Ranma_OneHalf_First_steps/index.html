Putting the bud in his ear Naota plugged the other end into the phone.

'I'm back." he said as he put the phone down. "And I can imagine why you're calling."

There was silence on the other end of the phone for a few moments before Matoko spoke.

"Kenji told you?" she asked.

'Yeah." Naota said with a nod as he picked the rifle back up.

'H-how is he?" Matoko asked.

"Right now he's sleeping after coming down from a caffeine bender, but other than that he's fine." answered Naota.

'He's...not ... he's not..." Matoko stammered.

"No, he's not going to run away." Naota said with a chuckle. "You just turned his world on end."

"I figured that." Matoko said. "I turned my world on end."

Naota actually laughed at that one as he pushed another patch through the bore of his rifle, then made a face as he saw what the patch looked like.

"Yuck." he said.

"What?" Matoko asked.

"Oh, I'm looking at the crud one of my toys picked up." Naota answered.

"Do I even want to know?" asked Matoko.

"Since this is an open line, no." Naota answered.

'Okay..." Matoko said. "Anyways, back to Kenji."

"Back to Kenji." repeated Naota. "Like I said, you turned his world on end."

"But I did do the right thing, right?" Matoko asked.

"Yeah." Naota answered. "You did. It'll just take him a while to accept the fact he really does like you."

"He does?" Matoko asked.

'Yeah." Naota said. "I mean, it shows in his artwork. You should take a look at his sketchbook sometime."

"His artwork? How so?" Matoko asked.

"Just idle sketches, stuff like that." Naota said. "He uses you for a lighting studies and stuff like that."

"Oh." Matoko said. "I thought it was because we worked together..."

"And I thought Lia and I were clueless." Naota said

'Ouch." Matoko said. "Are we that bad?"

'Yes." Naota said.

Matoko groaned and Naota heard the 'thump' as she flopped backwards. Naota chuckled as he looked down the muzzle of the barrel to check the bore.

"Matoko, let me give you a piece of advice. Just give Kenji some time." he said. " This is a whole new situation for him and it's gonna take him a while to get used to it."

"Okay." Matoko said. "So I should be prepared to wait awhile."

"Yeah." answered Naota. "But this is like producing a manga Matoko. It takes time and a lot of collaboration."

Matoko chuckled and Naota could hear the smile in her voice.

'Thanks Naota." she said. "It helped."

'Anytime." Naota answered. "Kenjis my brother. We gotta look out for each other."

"I'll talk to you later." Matoko said.

'Bye." Naota said before he disconnected.

Pulling the ear bud out he looked over at Kenjis bedroom, where snoring could be heard.

"Now all I gotta do is knock some sense into that head of yours Kenji.' he said with a sigh.

End.
