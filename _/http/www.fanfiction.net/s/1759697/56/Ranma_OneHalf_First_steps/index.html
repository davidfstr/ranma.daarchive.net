Ranma Onehalf: First Steps. Book Five.

Chapter 12.

Shrugging on her sleep shirt with a yawn, Chikako stepped out of her bedroom too check and make sure that the house was buttoned up before she went to bed. Checking the front door Chikako noticed that the wind had started to pick up.

"Odd..." she thought, then shrugged and continued on.

The rest of her check was completed in quick order and then she turned out the light and went to bed. Snuggling under the covers Chikako got comfortable and listened to the wind blowing outside. Soon her eyelids grew heavy and she drifted off to sleep.

In the shrine the charms and wards swayed slightly as the trees creaked and groaned outside as the wind began to pick up even more. Suddenly one of the wards near the alter began to crinkle up as if by an invisible hand before bursting into blue flames.

One by one the wards surrounding the alter burst into flames, then the rope they were hanging from snapped and flashed into flames as it fell to the floor. Soon nothing was left of the wards but ash.

For a few moments there was nothing but the wind, then there was the scrape of wood on wood and the ashes swirled as a shadow flowed out from the alter.

Clink!

Chikako stirred and muttered something about the wind as she rolled over. Snuggling up under the covers she started to drift back off to sleep when her her eyes flew open.

"Waitaminute -clink?"

Getting up on Lonee elbow Chikako rubbed her eyes and listened.

For a few moments there was nothing but the usual nighttime sounds, as the wind had had died down. Then there was the faintest creak of wood from somewhere within the house, and then Chikako knew what had woke her up: A burglar, albeit a suicidal one to be burgling a house in a ninja village.

Throwing the covers back she got up and padded over to her closet and slid the door open. Reaching inside Chikako quietly rummaged inside for a suitable weapon, and came up with two baseball bats. One was an Louisville Slugger, the other was a regular run -of -the -mill aluminum bat.

Deciding the Slugger was for special occasions, Chikako put it away and headed to the bedroom door. Sliding it open she peeked out down the hall and saw the coast was clear. Easing out of the bedroom she very carefully minded the nightingale floors(1) and snuck down the hall.

Coming to the living room Chikako peeked out around the corner and very carefully did a check of the room before she stepped into it.

Half way into the room there was a slight rustle and a creak behind her. Whirling around Chikako swung her bat at the noise, only nothing was there and she ended up hitting the wall.

Swearing under her breath Chikako fought to get her heart rate down as she stared at the hole in her wall. Making a mental note to call her cousin Eisaku in the morning in the morning to patch it, Chikako turned around to resume her search.

'Hi there."

Chikako shrieked as stumbled backwards from the shadowy figure in front of her. As the figure began to step towards her she regained her footing and brought the bat up in front of her like a sword.

The figure stopped and let out a low laugh. "Oooh, we know some kendo. I like that in a miko."

Chikako paused for a second as what the figure had said percolated through her brain. Hefting the bat, Chikako lunged forwards and swung at the figure who just simply sidestepped it. With a bang and crunch of plaster the head of the bat buried itself into the wall as the figure stepped behind her.

"And I love what they're doing with kimono's these days." said the figure as he stepped in close.

The next thing Chikako knew there was a hand clamped on her butt and five claws were poking her.

Letting out a scream of rage that would do an Amazon proud, Chikako whirled around and swung her bat at the intruders head- or, at least where she thought it would be. The figure leaned backwards and let the bat pass overhead, putting a third hole into Chikako's wall.

Pulling the bat free Chikako tried again and this time the intruder hopped backwards. That infuriated Chikako to no end, so she hefted her bat and lunged at him again with another scream.

And again he dodged her attack and this time it was a lamp that got hit, instead of the wall. Chikako tried again, and it was the floor. She did this three more times, hitting the couch, coffee table, and finally the TV.

The intruder whistled as he glanced at the imploded remains of the TV. "Wow. That looked expensive." he said as turned his gaze back to her. "Better keep practicing."

Chikako's left eye ticked and then she had an idea.

"Okay smart ass, dodge this." she growled.

Then she yanked her shirt up.

The intruder was starting to say something when she did that, but all that came out was an unintelligible mumble that was cut short by the crack of Chikako's bat connecting with his skull. With a scream of pain the intruder stumbled backwards as she reared back for another swing.

"THAT WASN'T FAIR- YAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHH!" the intruder screamed before Chikako cracked him in the ribs with her bat.

Now clutching his ribs with hone hand and his head with the other, the intruder continued to stumble around looking for an exit while trying not to get clobbered by Chikako. After a couple more solid thumps from Chikako he got lucky and tripped over a power cord, sending him headlong into a screen and out onto the porch.

Screaming in rage at the added damage to her house Chikako charged him, bat held high- only to hit the door jamb overhead.

Seeing his chance to escape, the intruder scrambled to his feet and ran as fast as he could. As he scrambled away Chikako could have sworn that she saw that he had tails. Lowering the bat Chikako sighed and walked back inside to clean up the mess.

Akahito Kawashima sighed as he wandered down a footpath away from the temple. The first girl he runs into after being unsealed, and she tries to bash his head in.

Reaching up, he felt his head and found the lump on his skull was almost gone, and by the feel of it any damage to his ribs was almost healed as well.

"Of course I had to go for the shrine maiden." Akahito grumbled as he came to a road.

And stopped. The road was nothing like he had ever seen before in his life -it was smooth for one, not cobbled. Ad it was lit by lamps. Or what he thought were lamps anyways. As he approached one he saw that it was high up on a pole, attached to a stone post.

With a shake of his tails, Akahito easily jumped up onto the beam and walked over to the lamp. On closer inspection he saw this was no ordinary lamp, for it contained no wick or candle, just a glowing ball of light. Shaking his head Akahito jumped down and continued down the road.

Ten minutes after he got into the town proper he was throughly lost. Nothing was the way he remembered. The building were completely changed, their architecture was completely unlike what he was used to.

After about half an hour of wandering Akahito found himself on top of a hill looking out over the town.

"Just how long have I been sealed?" he asked himself.

Dumping the last dustpan of plaster and TV fragments into the trash Chikako sighed and dropped the lid back onto the trashcan. Clipping the dustpan to the broom she put both back into the cupboard and then went to the sink to wash her hands.

Turning on the water she just happened to look up and see the back of the shrine through the kitchen window and remembered that she hadn't checked the shrine. With another sigh Chikako turned the water off and went to her bedroom to put some shorts on before she went outside.

A couple of minutes later she was at the shrine, flashlight in hand. Coming around to the front of the shrine Chikako saw that the door had been slid open, which meant that her recent visitor had been there. Stepping out of her sandals Chikako went into the shrine itself.

Running the beam of her flashlight over the interior she noticed that none of the decorations seemed to have been damaged or destroyed. She did notice that the wards in front of the shrine seemed to have been taken, but that appeared to be it.

Wondering why the hell someone would take the wards Chikako advanced further into the shrine and stopped when she felt something with the bare soles of her feet. Looking down she shined her flashlight at the floor and saw that there was ashes all over the floor, and in the ashes sandal prints leading from the alter.

Deciding that this warranted further examination Chikako headed to the alter. There she discovered that a previously hidden panel had been slid back.

"Now who would break into a shrine?" Chikako thought to herself. "The only thing rumored to have been there was- Chikako, you idiot."

Smacking herself on the head Chikako the realized that her intruder hadn't been breaking into the shrine, he had been breaking out of it. Shaking her head at not realizing it earlier, Chikako headed to where the scrolls were kept to check on them.

"I guess I did see some fox tails." she thought as she reached the storage space.

Opening it up Chikako saw that the scrolls were untouched. Sighing in relief, Chikako put her flashlight on a shelf and started to collect the scrolls.

"Might as well get these things to more secure place." she thought as she gathered up an armful. "The legend said he was here for these things in the first place when he was sealed, and it's only a matter of time before he remembers."

Akahito was relieved. After wandering for over an hour he had finally found a building he sort of recognized as an inn. He had found it after spotting a horseless, covered, metal cart going down the road.

Getting onto the roof of an adjacent building Akahito watched as boxes were unloaded from the cart into the inn. Why someone would make a delivery to an inn in the middle of the night, he didn't know. But then again, ever since he had been unsealed very little had made sense.

Since the occupants roof the building were busy unloading Akahito decided that this would be a good time to sneak into the building and see if he couldn't get a better handle on the current situation.

Slipping around to the alley alongside the inn wasn't much of a problem for him, nor was scaling the wall and jumping up to the second story. Sneaking along he found an window that opened up from what he remembered as a hallway. Slipping a claw into it he slid it open enough for him to enter and slipped inside.

And ran straight into a kunoichi.

Naota snapped awake at Tomoe's outraged shriek. Getting up, SOCOM in hand, Naota went to the door and stuck his head outside. Hearing what sounded like a major fight going on inside the main house Naota slipped outside and went into the main house.

Downstairs he ran into Ikuko, who was wielding a cast iron skillet.

"What's going on?" he asked.

Ikuko pointed at the ceiling with her skillet. "Intruder, Tomoe's room." she answered.

Naota nodded. "Ok. Go guard Lia." he ordered, ninja training kicking in.

Ikuko instantly complied with the order, her training kicking in as well. Going through the kitchen Naota got to the stairwell and started up it, SOCOM at the ready. When he reached the top of the stairs he saw Ryusake slumped against the wall amongst the shattered remains of a door, out cold.

Sneaking down the hall Naota saw Ryusake start to move and mentally put him under as 'okay'. Reaching the door Naota peeked around the jamb to see his fifteen year old cousin, clad only in a towel, trying to kill a kitsune with her ninja -to(2).

"Well, that explains the screaming." was the first thought that came to Naotas mind. "And it looks like Nerima isn't the only place where this sort of thing happens."

Of course thats when the towel decided to give in to gravity and come loose.

Getting an eyeful of skin the kitsune whistled appreciatively as Tomoe developed a facial tic.

"H-h-HENTAI!" shrieked Tomoe as she swung for the kitsune's head.

Out in the guest room Lia snapped awake at Tomoe's outraged shriek. As she came to Lia was aware of three things: One, her husband wasn't in bed with her. Two, it sounded like there was a brawl going on in the main house. And three, she could smell someone else in the room with her.

"W-who's there?" she asked, sitting up in bed.

A flicker of shadow, the slight creak of weight shifting on the floorboards. "Ikuko."

Blinking a couple of times to get the sleep out of her eyes, Lia was glad for the Neko -ken and her curse as the world resolved into the washed out tones that were her nightvision. She could see Naota's aunt, dressed in a pair of dark blue pajamas and wielding both a skillet and meat cleaver. Dressed and posed as she was, Ikuko looked like just another shadow -to someone without a Starlight scope(3) or the Neko -Ken Improved.

Throwing the covers off, Lia padded over to the door where Ikuko was crouched and listened to the fight raging inside.

"What's going on?" she whispered.

"Intruder." Ikuko whispered back.

Lia winced as a sudden scream followed by several obscenities pierced through the night. "Sounds like you got a pervert in there." she whispered, glancing over her shoulder at Ikuko.

With her nightvision Lia saw a smirk ghost Ikukos lips. "A suicidal pervert." she whispered. "All our local ones know better than to try anything with a kunoichi."

Listening to the mayhem raging inside the main house Lia smirked herself. "I can believe it." she said. "But, no offense, I don't think you guys have him under control."

Ikuko scowled as she edged closer to the door. "I can assure you, we ninja can handle a lone pervert." she whispered, trying not to sound testy.

"Then why-" Lia stopped when a loud pop followed by a snarl and cursing from Naota issued form the house, followed by the sounds of a body tumbling down the stairs. "Sounds like they got him." she said as she stood up.

"Wait! Don't go out there!" Ikuko hissed as she made a grab for Lia, her hands closing on empty air. "Shit!"

Lia was already going through the back door by the time Ikuko had stepped out of the guest house. "Wow, she's fast." was Ikuko's thought as she raced across the yard.

As Ikuko reached the door the kitchen lights came on, and she could hear Lia's voice coming from inside. As she turned the knob there was a scrabble of feet on linoleum, followed by the thump of two bodies hitting the floor.

Sliding the door open Ikuko heard a scream of rage and saw a flash. Her instincts screamed 'bomb' and took over, throwing her down and away from the door right before a body was sent smashing through the door.

Ikuko watched as the body -presumably the intruders- went flying through the air and smashed into a tree. Wincing at the impact Ikuko looked up as Lia stepped out onto the porch, her pigtail wreathing like a snake from her aura.

"Okay, I didn't know she could do that." Ikuko thought as Lia stalked towards the intruder, pulling a mallet from thin air. "Or that."

"You..." Lia ground out from between clench teeth. "You.." she repeated as the intruder groaned and shook his head.

Looking up the intruder saw Lia and blinked. Then he saw her mallet and his eyes bugged out to the size of dinner plates. "Ah, hey look, I'm sorry about running into you. I didn't mean to grab your-" he babbled.

"DIE!" Lia screamed as she reared back with the mallet. "HENTAI HAMMER BASH!" she screamed as s he swung, flooding the weapon with her supercharged chi.

Ikuko felt the whole house shake from the impact, and car alarms were set off for about a block around. The impact left a two and a half meter crater in the ground, but the intruder wasn't there.

He was landing in a crouch a few meters away, right next to the wall separating the Sano's property from their neighbors as lights were coming on all over the neighborhood. Upstairs a window was thrown open, bathing the backyard in light.

For a moment nothing happened, and that's when Ikuko got her first good look at the intruder. He was tall, about one seventy to one eighty centimeters(4), thin but well muscled. He was dressed in a black hakama and blue gray gi, with paired swords at his waist.

That wasn't too weird, just out of the ordinary. What stood out to Ikuko was the fact that he was barefoot, and his fingers ended in claws, not fingernails. And when he turned his head, she saw that he had pointed ears. This made a bell go off in her head, and the bell became an alarm when he turned slightly she saw he had four fox tails in the back.

"Mother Mary, he's loose! The Fox is loose!" she thought as she sat up.

"Now everyone stay calm..." the Fox began as Naota stepped out onto the porch and raised his pistol. "Lets not do anything rash- oh shit."

Kawashima quailed as the tai -yokai raised his tanegashima at him. He had heard about them, powerful weapons that could be wielded by the most common of humans and capable of taking down all but the most powerful yokai.

He knew that tanegashima had their limitations -they were slow to reload in comparison to a bow, and you had to keep the match lit so it could fire. But from the way everything had changed since he was sealed, Kawashima was sure that tanegashima had undergone similar changes as well.

So not wanting to find out what those changes were, he bolted. Out of the corner of his eye Kawashima saw the flash of the tanegashima firing and felt the tug of the ball passing through the sleeve of his gi. The tanegashima flashed again, and this time fire blossomed in his shoulder as the ball struck home.

Letting out a yelp of pain Kawashima leaped the fence as the tanegashima flashed a third time and fire lashed him in the thigh. Clearing the fence he landed in the yard and started running, trying to get as much space between him and the tanegashima wielding yokai as possible.

A few agonizingly long footsteps later he heard the crack of the tanegashima for the first time as a ball whipped past his ear. Two more followed in quick succession, one catching the leg of his hakama , the other missing him and hitting the ground in front of him.

Ahead of him was another wall, behind it he could see the roof of a shed poking up like a mushroom. From there he could see there was awning, which lead to the roof of the house of the house next door. Two more shots from the tanegashima and Kawashima's mind was made up for him.

Clutching his shoulder -that was going to take a while to heal- and Kawashima dropped and jumped, clearing the wall and landing on the roof of the shed. Two more leaps and he was on the roof of the house.

Hearing a frustrated snarl behind him Kawashima glanced back and saw the yokai leap from the shadow of the chimney behind him.

"Crap."

Kicking his legs into high gear he was off like a shot, the yokai in hot pursuit with the kunoichi and the red head trailing them.

Naota snarled as the intruder bounded across the rooftops. The last thing he was in the mood for was a steeple chase across Mabase at this hour of the night when he would much rather be in bed, with Lia.

Speaking of his mate, Naota could hear her behind him as she roofhopped along with him with Tome farther behind. From the way she went after the intruder Lia was pretty irate, and trying to discourage her from chasing him would be an exercise in futility.

And from what he could see Lia didn't have any trouble in going after him either. The only problem was what was going to happen when the intruder decided to quit playing nice and drew the katana at his waist. Lia had gotten better at dealing with sword attacks when she and Naota sparred, but there was a big difference between an oak bokken and live steel.

While Naota pondered this he up ahead he saw the intruder jump to one side suddenly as three kunai embedded themselves into the roof and two shadows separate themselves from the chimney. They had just ran into one of the Hagakure's night patrols.

In one fluid motion the intruder turned to face the attacking ninja and threw something at them from his left hand.

"Kitsune -bi!" the intruder shouted and a ridiculously over sized toy horse appeared between the ninja, catching them off guard and knocking them off the roof.

Naota could have sworn he heard the intruder snicker as he leaped to another roof. Filing it away for later, Naota leaped over the horse and continued on his chase. A couple of seconds later a thump told him that Lia had cleared the horse, followed a few seconds ofter that by Tomoe.

Glancing back to make sure they were okay Naota looked forwards and saw that they were rapidly running out of houses. Looking to his right Naota saw two teams of ninjas running parrelled to then on the other side of the street, and to the left there was another team of ninjas doing the same thing.

Running through his mental map of Mabase Naota reviewed on what probably lay in front of them: some fields, a small woods, and... the temple. That made Naota smile. They were herding him into a trap.

Reaching the end of the houses the intruder hopped down to ground and headed in the direction of the woods at a dead sprint. Hitting the ground Naota started to accelerate when he saw the intruder suddenly slam into an invisible wall and get thrown back with a flash of light.

Skidding to a stop a few meters away Naota looked around and saw what he was looking for: ofuda stuck to fencepost's, creating a barrier. Further checking revealed other ofuda stuck to sticks driven into the ground around them, making an open -ended box.

Putting his hand to his wounded shoulder the intruder sighed and looked around the area. "Alright miko, show yourself." he yelled out to the night. "If your going to seal me away again, at least have the courage to come out and show your face while you do it."

There was a rustle in the grass and Chikako stepped out from behind the bush that was her hiding place. She was dressed in a t -shirt and shorts, and was carrying a quiver and bow. Taking in her appearance the intruder snorted and looked at Naota.

"And you let her control you, a tai -yokai?" he asked, nodding at Chikako.

Narrowing his eyes Naota raised his pistol as Lia and Tomoe came up behind them. "She doesn't control me, fox, and worry about me, not her." he said as he settled the laser dot on the forehead of the intruder. "So, what's your name, and why are you here?"

Sighing again the intruder looked down at his shoulder, then back up to Naota. "My name is Akahito Kawashima, and the reason I am here is because I was asked by my sister Asako to check on some scrolls." he said as Naota's ears perked up.

"Your sister's name is Asako?" he asked.

Akahito nodded. "Yeah." he said. "She's about yea high-" Akahito held up his hand at about Asako's hight "-is really cheerful and nice, has four tails, likes to drive Shessy -poo nuts?"

Now Naota nodded and flipped on the safety of his pistol. "That's her." he said, sticking the gun in his waistband. Hearing the scrape of wood on wood, Naota glanced over at Chikako as she nocked an arrow. "Just what are you doing?" he asked.

"Re -sealing him." Chikako answered, her eyes never leaving Akahito. "Before he causes any more trouble."

Seeing Akahito quail in terror out of the corner of his eye Naota frowned and shook his head. "Hold off on that, would ya Chikako?" he asked.

Lowering her bow Chikako turned and faced Naota. "Why?" she asked incredulously.

"Because I'm a jonin, and because I said so." answered Naota. As Chikako spluttered he turned to Tomoe. "Tomoe, are there any spare rooms at the house?" he asked.

She nodded once. "Yeah. Downstairs, there's a walk -in closet." she said.

"Good." Naota said as he pointed to Chikako. "Take her and have her ward it. The fox can stay there for the time being."

"What?" asked Akahito, totally confused.

"WHAT?" shrieked Lia, Tomoe, and Chikako, totally outraged.

"You're sparing him?" shrieked Tomoe. "He snuck into my room when I was changing!"

"By accident!" Akahito called.

"And he snuck into my house and groped me!" Chikako yelled.

"He groped me too!" Lia yelled.

"Hey!" Naota yelled, his voice rising above the others. "I'm not saying you cant beat the crap out of him-!" he continued.

"What?" Akahito yelped.

"-just not kill him or seal him."finished Naota.

"Oh." the three women said before turning their gaze to Akahito.

Sweating nervously Akahito looked at Naota. "Ah, why are you leaving me to them?" he asked.

Looking over his shoulder Naota leveled his gaze at Akahito. "Because you interrupted my sleep, that's why." he answered. "Live with it."

Akahito swallowed nervously as Lia, Chikako, and Tomoe approached him. "Help?" he asked weakly.

Feeling sunlight hit her eyes, Bre groaned and rolled over. She lay that way for a few moments until she realized that something was wrong -the other half of the bed was empty. Flopping her arm around failed to locate a certain soldier, so reluctantly Bre cracked open an eye.
