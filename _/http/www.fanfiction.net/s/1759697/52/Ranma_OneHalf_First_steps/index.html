Ranma Onehalf: First steps. Book Five.

Chapter 8.

Lia lay very still and tried not to think. Thinking hurt. Her head was pounding, her eyes were glued shut, her tounge needed shaved, her stomach was doing flip -flops, and her mouth tasted like a sewer.

Lia, was, in short, hung over. Badly.

The sound of footsteps in the hall made Lia grimace in pain and for once wish she didn't have such sensitive hearing. Eventually the footsteps stopped to which Lia sighed in relief and immediately regretted it.

When the latch on her door popped open it was like Naota had fired his pistol. Lia grimaced again as the footsteps started again, this time louder and coming into her room.

They stopped when they were right next to her. Cracking open one eye Lia was given a very blurry view of something green and brown right in front of her.

"green?" Lia thought. Blinking a couple of times it resolved into an old green tang and wavy dark brown and Lia realized why it was there: Matoko was wearing it. "Matoko." she thought as she remembered.

After helping heave last night they had gone back to her room and changed into something a little more comfortable than evening wear. And since her nightgown was such a long ways away, Matoko had simply grabbed one of Lias sleep shirts.

That mystery solved Lia very carefully tilted her head back and looked up to see Bre standing over her and Matoko and looking entirelytoo cheerful, to which Lia gave her a look that said 'if you're not here to put me out of my misery then go away and let me die in peace'.

"Rise and shine." Bre whispered, which was still too loud for Lia. "You've got to get married in a couple of hours." she added.

Giving a slight sigh Lia rolled away from Matoko and , careful not to upset her stomach, sat up. The movement brought Matoko around too, who opened her eyes to get an up -close view of Bres feet. Blinking a couple of times she rolled over and winced at the fresh pain in her head.

Sitting up herself Matoko rubbed her forehead.

"What did I drink...?" she mewed.

"I told you that that you'd regret that Cuervo." Bre answered, to which Matoko groaned. "And Akane said that when you two are ready get downstairs and she'll make some hangover cures for you."

Lia groaned in reply while Matoko started to nod, then stopped. Her eyes bugged out as she clamped a hand over her mouth, lunged for the trash can by Lias desk and retched.

Bre grimaced and hurried over to Matoko. "Here, let me hold your hair." she said. As Matoko went into the dry heaves Bre looked at Lia, who was getting a little green around the gills. "Think you can make it to the bathroom?" Bre asked.

Lia nodded once as Matoko finished up. "How about you?" she asked. "Can you make it?"

Getting a nod Bre helped Matoko to her feet as rolled onto hers and ran to the bathroom as fast as she could. Behind her came Matoko and Bre.

Bre left them in the bathroom and headed downstairs, where the wounded were gathered in various stages of being hung -over. Stepping around Nabiki -who looked like she had caught the T- Virus- Bre went to the kitchen and poked her head in.

Inside the kitchen Kasumi was washing out the blender in the sink while Akane sipped her hangover cure(1). She looked up when Bre stuck her head in.

"The bride will be down in a minute." Bre said. "right now Matokos holding her hair."

Akane nodded gently, mindful of her headache. "Kay." she said in a low voice. "I'll get started on their Hangover Tonics(2)."

Bre chuckled as she stepped into the kitchen. "Man, that was some party last night." she said.

Akane laughed as she walked over to the freezer. "I know." she said. "I didn't know Matoko could hold her liquor like that."

Bre shrugged as she found a place to stand by the phone. "She's a big girl, Akane." she said. "Yeah." Akane said as she took out a tub of ice cream. Shutting the freezer she took a bottle of seltzer out of the fridge and shut the door. "But she has no meat on her." she added.

"I noticed that too." Kasumi interjected as she handed the clean blender to Akane. "Has she been eating?"

Taking the blender Akane nodded. "Yes, she has." she said. "As much as Christian and Mamimi. But it doesn't stick." Akane added as she pried the lid off the ice cream. "Honestly, I think she could clean out a buffet and lose weight."

Bre snorted as she leaned against the wall. "I wish I had that problem." she said.

"There is a solution for that." Akane said as she glanced over her shoulder. "All you have to do is practice the Anything Goes chi techniques for the next twenty years and you'll be set." she said as she dug a scoop out of the drawer.

"No thanks." Bre said as she shook her head.

Akane shrugged as she scooped out some ice cream an dumped it into the blender. "Your loss." she said.

After she had put the ice cream into the blender Akane poured in some seltzer, then went back to the fridge for some milk. After adding that she gritted her teeth against the pain and pressed 'frappes. She let it run for about forty -five seconds, then turned it off. Massaging her forehead to clear the pain Akane took two large glasses from the cupboard and set them on the counter. Hearing the slow 'thump-thump' of feet on the stairs Akane pried the lid off the blender.

"Sounds like the girls are done with the bathroom." said Akane as she poured the milkshakes. "Could you take these out to them?" she asked, which got a nod from Bre.

Sticking a straw in each of them, Akane handed the milkshakes to Bre who then took them into the main room where Lia and Matoko were seated at the table.

"Here you go girls." Bre said as she set the shakes in front of them. "Two hangover cures."

Lia blinked as she looked at the milkshake i n front of her, then turned her gaze at Bre. "How come you're not hung over?" she asked as Matoko sipped hers.

"Sanos are immune to hangovers." Bre answered with a smile. "Something I think the boys are gonna find out soon."

Sitting up Naota stretched and looked around the room he was sleeping in. It was quite clearly the Tofu family living room, and sprawled around him were several passed out men and one woman. Hearing movement in the kitchen Naota got up and found Evans in there with Ono making coffee.

"You're up." Evans rasped when Naota came in.

Naota nodded as he rubbed the back of his neck. "Yeah." he answered. "And you look like hell." he added as he looked over Evans.

"Yeah, well, some of us aren't like Sanos and are immune to hangovers." Evans replied, to which Naota snorted in amusement.

"Thats not my problem." Naota responded. "And speaking of problems, where's Dad and Grandpa?"

"They left an hour ago." Ono said as he pulled some food from the fridge. "Right before Evans woke up."

Naota nodded as he looked back into the living room and the passed out partygoers in there. "Oh. Did they say where they were going?" he asked.

Closing the fridge door with his foot Ono maneuvered the containers of food over to the counter. "To Lias house." he said. "Something about setting up security for the wedding."

"Oh, thats right, Uncle Hideki an' the rest of the family got in last night." Naota said with a shake of his head.

Evans gave Naota an odd look. "Do you know just how odd that sounds?" he asked.

Naota nodded. "Yeah." he answered. "But this is Nerima." added Naota. "Having ninjas run security for your wedding is a necessity."

Evans winced at that. "The scary part is that you're right." he said as the coffee finished perking.

"Tell me about it." Naota answered. "By the way, how are we gonna get the stiffs in there to house for the wedding?" he asked.

"Well..." Evans said as he poured himself some coffee. "As soon as I have some coffee I'm gonna give them a sample of what basic was like."

"Ah." Naota said with a nod. "That should be fun." he added. "But be sure to leave some coffee for Kenji. He'll need it."

"Oh, he slow getting up in the morning?" Evans asked over his cup.

Naota snorted in amusement. "A zombie would have nothing on him." he responded as Ono chuckled.

"Then he sounds like 'Biki(3) and Chisu." he said as he finished his breakfast. "I assume he takes his coffee black?" asked Ono.

Naota shook his head. "Nah. Normally he drinks tea." he said. "But his tea can eat through most plastics."

Evans and Ono winced at that statement. "Sounds like the coffee at the office." Evans said before he drained his cup. "And now, it's time to go deliver a wake up call." he said as he cracked his knuckles.

Striding into the living room Evans cleared his throat and set to work.

"WAKE UP!" he bellowed. "WE DON'T HAVE TIME FOR YOUR BEAUTY SLEEP LADIES!"

The effect was instantaneous. Immediately all the bodies came to life, groaning and rolling over in an effort to escape the assault on their eardrums. Unfortunately, that proved to be an exercise in futility, for Evans was not to be deterred.

Rolling over with a groan Hideo looked up at Evans. "Do you mind? Some of us are trying to sleep." he rasped.

Leaning over Hideo Evans glowered down at Hideo. "IF I WANTED YOUR OPINON I'D ASK FOR IT MAGGOT! NOW GET ON YOUR FEET! MOVE! MOVE! MOVE!' bellowed Evans.

That had the desired effect: Hideo was instantly on his feet and at attention. In short order Evans had everyone else up, on their feet, and outside the house. Shoving everyone into a rough formation Evans was far from happy with how it looked, but it'd do.

"ALRIGHT MAGGOTS! LISTEN UP!" he bellowed. "WE'RE HEADING TO THE SAOTOME HOUSE, AND IF I HEAR ANY BITCHING OR WHINING WERE DOING DOUBLE TIME AND EVERYONE GIVES ME FIFTY WHEN WE GET THERE! IS THAT CLEAR!" Evans yelled. Getting a chorus of groans he scowled. "I CANT HEAR YOU!"

"Sir, yes sir." came the weak reply.

"THAT'S BETTER!" Evans bellowed in reply. "NOW LETS GO! FORWARD, MARCH!"

With that, the motley group trudged forwards.

Once they were done with her hangover cure Lia was hustled off by Nodoka to tent that had been set up alongside the house so she could begin getting into her wedding kimono. Matoko came along as well to help Lia as well.

That left Nabiki at the table, her hangover making her even more sluggish than normal. She had finished her tonic and was now working her way through her third cup of coffee.

Nabiki was sitting with her eyes closed, letting the caffeine work its way into her system when Bre came in.

"I'm gonna be helping out in the dojo. You need anything?" asked Bre. Getting a shake of the head from Nabiki Bre nodded. "Okay. Akane and Kasumi are in the kitchen if you need anything." she said as she left.

Cracking open her eyes Nabiki watched as Bre left, then sighed and laid her head on the table. The sounds of the curtain being moved and heavy footsteps told her that Akane had come in.

"Hey 'Kane." Nabiki said, her voice muffled.

"Hey 'Biki." Akane answered as he sat down. "You okay?"

Nabiki sniffled and lifted her head. "No." she answered as tears slid down her cheeks, streaking the mascara she hadn't bothered to remove last night. "I'm not."

Clearly surprised to see Nabiki crying, Akane leaned forwards towards his sister. "Whats wrong?" he asked.

Nabiki chewed on her lower lip for a second, then took a slug of coffee before she responded. "Bre." she answered. Getting an odd look from Akane Nabiki continued. "She sounds exactly like her mother."

"You knew her mother?" Akane asked. "I didn't know that. Why didn't you say anything?"

Nabiki sniffled again and wiped her nose with the sleeve of her bathrobe. "Two reasons." she said. "One, it never seemed to be the right time, and two,-" Nabiki swallowed again before continuing. "And two it brings up a lot of painful memories for me. Haruko was my best friend."

Akane sipped his hangover cure as he thought about what his sister had said. "How come we never met her?" he asked, the 'we' referring to the family.

Nabiki laughed mirthlessly. "You know what I was like when we were kids." she said. "I was the Ice Queen, remember? I had an image to maintain at home."

"Ah." Akane said with a nod. "But I take it theres more, isn't it?" he asked, to which Nabiki nodded.

"Kamone." Nabiki said. "He was never intimidated by me. And that bugged the hell out of me." she continued. "So I tried to dig up dirt on him. Second biggest mistake of my life."

"Okay." Akane said with a nod.

"I get a note one day in class. It's from him, asking to meet him after school. Alone." Nabiki said, staring down at the empty coffee cup in her hands. "I show up, and he hands me an envelope. In it were photos of our family. Asleep, at night. He told me that if I left him alone, he'd leave me alone."

"at...night?" Akane asked, a bit of a tremor in her voice.

Nabiki nodded as she continued. "And everything was fine, till a couple of months later he and Haruko met." she said. "And we left each other alone, and everything was fine. He didn't try to come between us or anything, and I didn't tell Haruko what I felt." Nabiki continued as she pushed the mug away as she stretched. "Which was that he was dangerous, and she might get hurt. Turns out I was right, but it wouldn't be because of him."

Akane leaned on the table and looked at his sister. "So what happened?" he asked.

Nabiki buried her face in her hands as she spoke. "Before I tell you that, you've got to understand something. When I said we were best friends, I wasn't kidding. I was there when Bre and Naota were born. She was there when Chisu was born- in fact it was her that kept me from strangling Tatewaki when I was in labor." she said with a laugh, then she sobered. "I consoled her when Bre was put up for adoption, and shared her joy when she found out she was pregnant with Naota. Thats how we were, okay?"

Akane nodded as his sister continued. "You and Ranma were gone when she died. There had been a series of assaults on housewives a couple of wards over, and, and," Nabiki swallowed before she said the next part. "And Kamone had taken Naota to the zoo on his fifth birthday. And their apartment got hit." she said quietly. "Haruko fought back, and the bastard killed her. He had to choose that day to change areas."

They sat in silence for a couple of moments before Akane finally spoke. "So thats what happened." she said. "and why you've seemed distant."

"Uh-huh." Nabiki answered. "And ever since I met Bre I keep getting reminded of what happened."

Akane looked down at his glass as he considered what to say next. Finally he looked up at his sister. "So, what are you going to do?"he asked.

Nabiki sighed and shrugged. "Well, I'm going to tell Bre and Naota what I told you after the wedding." she said as she looked at her hands again. "And I'm going to apologize to Kamone. I said some things to him that I shouldn't have when Haruko died." Nabiki added. "And I have something that belongs to Bre and Naota too."

"What is it?" asked Akane.

Nabikis mouth quirked into a smile. "All of Harukos work." she said. "I was archiving it onto disk when she died. I made a copy for myself, and Bre and Naota are getting copies as well, and I'm gonna give the originals to Kamone." added Nabiki as she got up. "But, for now, I'm going to get off my rear and go help get my niece married."

"Okay." Akane said as she stood up. "And I'm heading back into the kitchen. I think the guys are here and they're going to need lots and lots of tonic."

"So, what do you think?"

"I look ridiculous."

Naota was standing in Akanes sewing room dressed in his wedding kimono. It was navy blue, made out of the best silk they could get and had tigers embroidered on it in dark silver. All in all, it was a very handsome and elegant garment to be married in.

"Nonsense." Dokuhotei said as he admired his grandson. "You dignified and noble, like a great samurai."

Naota scowled at his grandfathers reflection in the full length mirror he was standing in front of. "Grandpa, we're Hagakure. We killed samurai, not looked like them." he said as he fanned himself. "And could you get a fan? I'm roasting here."

His kimono was also exactly the wrong thing to be wearing in the middle of a Tokyo summer, a fact not lost on Dokuhotei who was also dressed in his own formal kimono.

"I'll go see what I can scrounge up." he said as he walked to the door. "At the least I'll see if Kasumi has any of that lemonade left."

Naota nodded his thanks as his grandfather left. Standing there roasting in his kimono Naota wondered how soon it was going to be before the wedding proper started, and if he would have died of heat exhaustion by then.

His right ear twitched when he head footsteps in the hall, his demonic hearing and years of training in the Hagakure school telling him that it was Bre approaching.

"Knock knock." Bre said as she knocked on the door frame.

"Hey Bre." Naota said with a wave.

"Hey kitten." Bre said as she stepped in. "Nervous?"

Naota laughed nervously as he looked around the room. "You have no idea." he said. "I've was calmer when I faced down the school." Naota said as he looked up at his sister. "You know, this is just a formality, cause Lia and I are mated. So why am I so nervous?"

Bre chuckled as she began to dig around in the pockets of her jeans. "Probably because it just hit home that you're going to be spending the rest of your life with this person?" she said as she found what she was looking for. "On that thought, Kamone planned ahead and told me to give you this." Bre said as she pulled a hip flask from her pocket and handed it to Naota.

Naota sighed in relief as he took the flask from his sister. "Thank god, I could use a belt right now." he said as he unscrewed the cap. After taking a healthy swig he put the cap back on and tucked it into his kimono. "You know, every now and then that idiot actually does something right."

"So you're still mad at him." Bre said as she crossed her arms.

"No I'm not." Naota said with a shake of his head. "I'm punting him into the walls anymore, am I?" he added as Bre gave him a sour look. "Look, he's here in the wedding, okay?'

Bre sighed as she looked at her brother. "So when are you going to start talking to him?" she asked.

"As soon as I confirm some recessive family traits with Uncle Hideki." Naota answered as he pulled the flask out again.

"What family traits?" Bre asked as she put her hands on her hips. "What are you talking about?"

The flask paused halfway at his lips as Naota responded. "You haven't met Uncle Hideki?" he asked.

"No."

Naota nodded as he took a quick belt from the flask. "So you wouldn't know." he said as he put the flask back on.

"Know what Naota?" Bre asked as she stamped her foot in irritation.

Naota shrugged as he put the flask away again. "Uncle Hideki is our fathers twin brother." he said. "Grandpa is a twin, back home in the mountains(4) we have at least six cousins that are twins. It's a family trait.

Bre blanched as she absorbed this information. "You mean..." she began.

Naota shrugged. "We could have twin siblings out there." he said. "But Grandpa said that it was recessive with Dad and Uncle Hideki. They're identical twins, so since his kids aren't..."

Bre blinked. "Waitaminute. If you already know, why verify?" she asked.

Naota shrugged. "Paranoia, that and we have a cousin who I owe a few dozen noogies to." he answered as he cracked his knuckles. "Besides, his wifes an incredible cook and I want to get her, Matoko, and Kasumi together in the kitchen."

Bre rolled her eyes as she looked at her brother. "Do you always have an ulterior motive for everything?" she asked.

"Please." Naota said with a wave of his hand. "I have at least two, with back up motives as well not to mention a plan B, C, and D."

"What ever." Bre said as she shook her head. "I'm gonna go get changed." she said.

"Kay."Naota said with a nod. "And tell Grandpa to hurry up with that lemonade. I'm roasting here." he said

"I will." Bre said as she left.

Walking back down the hall Bre was completely unprepared for the sight that greeted her: the kitsune Asako Kawashima having tea with Chris- chan and Mamimi in matching kimonos, with Asakos kimono looking like it belonged in a Edo period courtesans wardrobe.

Asako looked up when Bre came in and gave her a smile that promised lots of mischief.

"Hello Bre." she said. "I would have gotten here earlier, but I was needed at the shop."

Bre blinked twice, then shook her head. "You got invited to the wedding?" she asked.

Asakos smile grew into a huge grin as she nodded. "Uh -huh. Kamone needed a date." she answered.

Bre nodded. "Ohhh -kay. Naota is gonna kill him for that, but thats nothing new." she said as she eyed Asakos kimono. "Nice kimono."

"This old thing?" asked Asako as she looked down at herself. "I've had it for centuries." she said. "And that reminds me."

Bre watched as Asako snapped her fingers and she was bathed in foxfire for a moment. When it cleared her hair was red, her eyes were fox -looking, and she now had six large fox tails attached to her rear. Looking up at Bre Asako sighed in relief.

"ahhh... much better." Asako said.

Bre blinked as she saw that Asako had what looked like whisker markings on her face. "Naota and Lia weren't kidding about you being a fox." she said.

"Nope." Asako answered as her grin returned. Swishing her tails she continued. "I'm 100 kitsune." she said as she pointed at Christian. "Though Christian here would make a decent fox too. All he needs is some tails."

Bres eyes bugged out as Asako said that and she turned to head up the stairs. "Ah, yeah. I gotta go change. Bye." she said before fleeing.

Asako watched Bre flee before turning back to Chris -chan and Mamimi. "What a thoroughly odd girl." she said.

"I think it's cause she's an American." Christian said as she sipped her (cold) tea. "Her, Evans, my sister. They're all kinda weird like that."

"Oh well." Asako said as she refilled her tea. "Now, about the finer points of mischief."

Inside the kitchen Lia sat on a stool, trying not to chew on her lip and thus smudge her lipstick. She was dressed in the traditional all-white wedding kimono, her hair was set into the bunkin -takashimada, and she the slightest bit of makeup on it. Grandma Nodoka had insisted on the full workup, but a hissing fit from Lia had put that to rest.

With Lia in the kitchen were Matoko and Akane. Her sister was dressed in a indigo kimono with gold phoenixes while Akane was in cursed form wearing a dark red kimono with dragons picked out in black. They were both standing, lost in their own thoughts while they waited with her.

Unable to stand it anymore Lia looked up at her mother.

"Mom, what was your wedding like?" she asked.

"mm?" Akane grunted as he was snapped out of his thoughts by Lias question. "My wedding?" he asked. "It wasn't a wedding so much as a ceremony with a justice of the peace at the Blackhawk County Courthouse." he added.

"Oh." Lia said as she looked down at her hands.

Seeing the way his daughters tail twitched from side to side Akane could tell that Lia was nervous- understandable, given that she was going to get married in a few minutes.

"You okay?" he asked. "Can I get you something?"

Lia shook her head slightly as she looked up at her mother. "No, thanks." she said. Taking a deep breath she continued. "Did you feel like you wanted to throw up when you got married?" Lia asked.

Akanes mouth quirked into a smile as he recalled the memory. "Once or twice." he said. "Ranma did throw up two or three times, but that's cause he was carrying you, so..." Akane added with a shrug.

"Oh. Well thats not why my stomachs upset." Lia said as she looked around the kitchen. "I think it's the hangover and adrenaline." she said. "But that bag of catnip Bre tossed into the tent helped."

"Thank god for that." Akane said. "Otherwise I don't think we would have never gotten Christian dressed, even with Matoko and Mamimis help."

Matoko looked up at the mention of her name. "Mmm?" she asked. "Is it time?" she asked.

Akane looked up at the clock and nodded. "Just about." he said. "You ready Lia?" he asked.

"Yeah." Lia said as she stood up. "I'm as ready as I'll ever be." she added.

"Well, lets go." Akane said as he held the curtain up for Lia. "It's time we got you married."

With slow, deliberate steps, Lia walked out of the kitchen and into the main room. Turning left she headed down the hall with Akane in front and Matoko at the rear. Passing by the sewing room Lia could have sworn she heard Dokuhotei muttering about how Naota hadn't left anything for him, but then they were at the door to the dojo.

Sliding the door open Akane stepped inside, then over to the right to make way for her. Inside the dojo was was empty, save for Lama Eno who was kneeling on the floor as he waited for the bride and groom. The screens were opened and outside stood all of Lia and Naotas friends and family, similarly dressed in colorful kimonos as well.

The only exceptions were Bre and Evans, Evans being dressed in his Class A's and Bre in a simple -yet elegant – a -line dress in a medium purple. Her hair was in a very elaborate set of braids, showing that even when she was dressed up she was still a punk at heart.

Beside Bre Evans stood as a dark green monolith, his ribbons, patches, and rank insignia adding a splashplash of color.

Lia walked across the dojo floor and knelt down next to Lama Eno, and a few moments later Naota appeared and joined her. This being his cue Lama Eno stood up and cleared his throat.

"Today, Saotome Lia and Sano Naota are happy not just because they can express their love for each other with friends and family, but also because they can express their aspirations for the future." he said. Looking down at the two kneeling at his feet Lama Eno nodded and they both rose and turned to face him.

"Naota and Lia, do you pledge to help each other to help develop your hearts and minds, cultivating compassion, generosity, ethics, patience, enthusiasm, concentration, and wisdom as you age and undergo the various ups and downs of life and to transform them into the path of love, compassion, joy, and equanimity?"

Green eyes met blue and found the answer to the question in each other.

"We do." said Naota and Lia together.

"Recognizing that the external conditions in life will not always be smooth and that internally your own minds and emotions will sometimes get stuck in negativity, do you pledge to see all these circumstances as a challenge to help you grow, to open your hearts, to accept yourselves, and each other; and to generate compassion for others who are suffering? Do you pledge to avoid becoming narrow, closed or opinionated, and to help each other to see various sides of situations?"

As the Lama spoke Naota and Lias thoughts went over their past two tumultuous years together and found their answer.

"We do." they said.

"Understanding that just as we are a mystery to ourselves, we are a mystery to each other. Do you pledge to seek to understand yourselves, each other, and all living things? Do you pledge to continually examine your own minds and to regard all the mysteries of life with both curiosity and joy?"

"We do." Lia and Naota answered.

"Do you pledge to preserve and enrich your affection for each other, and to share it with all beings? Totake the loving feelings you have for one another and your vision of each other's potential and inner beauty as anexample and rather than spiraling inwards and becoming self absorbed, to radiate this love outwards to allbeings?"

"We do."

"When it comes time to part, do you pledge to look back at your time together with joy-joy that you met and shared what you have-and acceptance that we cannot hold on to anything forever?"

In the audience Soun, Dokuhotei, and Kamone sighed softly as Naota and Lia spoke.

"We do." they said.

"Do you pledge to remember the disadvantages of ignorance, anger and clinging attachment, to apply antidotes when these arise in your minds, and to remember the kindness of all other beings and your connection to them? Do you pledge to work for the welfare of others, with all of your compassion, wisdom and skill?"

Each remembered the lessons learned from their childhood and nodded.

"We do." they said.

"Do you pledge to work to develop the wisdom understanding the relative functioning nature of things and the wisdom knowing their deeper way of existence that they are empty of inherent existence? And to remember the laws of cause and effect?"

Lia felt her tail as it swayed softly from side to side, and Naota smelled the rich tapestry of scents in the room and they both nodded.

"We do." they said.

"Do you pledge day to day, to be patient with yourselves and others, knowing that change comes slowly and gradually, and to seek inspiration from your teachers not to become discouraged?"

They both remembered the long hours spent in the dojo as they nodded.

"We do." they said.

"Do you pledge to continuously strive to remember your own Buddha nature, as well as the Buddha nature of all living beings? To maintain the awareness that all things are temporary, and to remain optimistic that you can achieve your greatest potential and lasting happiness?"

The discoveries that they had each made in the last years came back to them as the Lama spoke.

"We do." they said.

Lama Eno nodded and put his hands together as he spoke to them.

"Then the ring that you have for each other are a sign of the inward and spiritual bond which unites your two hearts in partnership." he said as Naota and Lia turned to each other.

Reaching into his kimono Naota took out a plain gold wedding band, and taking Lias hand placed it on her ring finger. After he was done Lia reached into her hakoseko (6) and took out a identical, but larger, ring and placed it on his ring finger.
