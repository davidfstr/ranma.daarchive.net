Currently she fidgeting nervously as she watched the buildings roll by outside the smoked bulletproof glass of the windows. Outwardly, she looked a trifle nervous, but on the inside she was a gibbering wreck.

The night before, she'd had to track down two foxes considerably more powerful than them and tell them what to do. True, she'd had her Lord's authority behind her at the time, but it was still very intimidating. But as intimidating as that had been, compare to her final task those had been nothing.

She was to relay instructions to Asako Kawashima, daughter of Kyuubi Kawashima and then submit herself to Asako to quote 'learn from her on how to be a kitsune' unquote.

Asako Kawashima was the leading cause for gossip and rumor about the Kawashima family. The mildest thing she had ever did was make a complete and utter fool of the wolf Koga by getting him blind drunk, then putting him into bed with the Tanyuki lord's two daughters.

Koga's wife had been incensed by the fact that Asako had humiliated her and her husband in such a public fashion, and had gone after Asako. Ten hours later, beaten, burned, bruised, dirty, and naked, Koga's wife had thrown in the towel and gone home.

And she was supposed to be her teacher?

Nervously, Jun swallowed as the car glided to a stop in front of a store. Rolling down the window Aki turned around to look at her.

"We're here." he said.

Nodding jerkily, Jun took several deep breaths to steady her nerves as Aki got out and went to the passenger side door and opened it for her. Scooting over she was helped out of the car by him and then she headed to the door, Aki about three steps behind her.

"W- why are y-you following m -me?" asked Jun as she glanced nervously over her shoulder.

Aki shrugged as he gave the street a once over. "Lord Kyuubi said I was supposed to be security." he said. "That's what I'm doing."

Jun nodded jerkily and swallowed. "O -okay." she said as she stepped towards the door. "T-t-thank's."

Aki chuckled to himself as Jun paused at the door, took a breath, and knocked. Then waited. And waited some more. After about five minutes Jun was reaching to knock again when the blinds behind the door parted at eye height.

"Aki! Jun! What brings you guys here?" she asked, her eyes narrowing. "You need a little 'something something?'"

"As a matter of fact, he is!" Kamone said. "And so is his friend. They're out back having tea with Dad and Bre. I'm sure he'd love to see his niece and nephew." he added, stepping around the counter to open the pass through for them.

Ai and Honzo looked up at their father for approval and he gave them a nod. Following behind them he tried to place the name 'Bre' before. As he navigated them through the back of the shop through the machinery he spotted a guitar pick lying incongruously on a table and thats when he remembered who Bre was: Naotas sister.

Mentally kicking himself, he opened the door to the area that the Sanos used as a combination break room/storeroom and ushered his children in.

Inside the breakroom sat Kenji and Dokuhotei, playing shogi while Bre and Matoko sat off to the side and conversed.

"hey, String bean" Takata said as he came in. "Look who I brought."

"huh?" Startled, Kenji blinked and looked up. His eyes got wide when he saw who was accompanying his brother. "Oh my."

"Hello." Dokuhotei said, turning in his seat to meet the children, shogi momentarily forgotten. "And who might you two be?" he asked as Bre and Matoko came over.

There was an awkward silence as both of the children shied back from Dokuhotei, which didn't surprise Takata at all. Ai and Honzo had always been very perceptive about people, and for all of his charm and general goofiness, Dokuhotei was a very dangerous man.

"Ah, Kenji, why don't you and the girls take them out for some ice cream?" Takata suggested, breaking the silence.

"huh -oh. Sure!" Kenji said,glancing back at Matoko. "What do you think, Matoko?" he asked.

Matoko gave him a gentle smile and nodded slightly. "I'd love to." she said. "There's a new pistachio nut flavor that I've been wanting to try for a while."

Bre's stomach gave a hungry rumble, to which she could only grin sheepishly. "I guess I don't have much choice in the matter." she said.

Takata stood aside as the party left the room, with Kamone following. "I'll be watching the front." he said before closing the door behind him.

Dokuhotei listened until he hear the bells above the door jangle, then looked at Takata. "So." he said, motioning to the seat recently vacated by Kenji. "What brings you here? It's not for a social visit."

Takata shrugged and sat down. "It's my wife." he said looking down at the board, analyzing the layout of the pieces.

"What about her?" asked Dokuhotei, gazing at the board. "It's your move." he added.

Takata glanced up at Dokuhotei for a moment, then focused on the board. "I want a divorce." he answered as he moved a piece.

Dokuhotei watched Takata move the piece he had selected, then arched an eyebrow. Interesting.

"I'm a devout Catholic." he said, moving one of his pieces so it potentially threatened two of Takatas. "We aren't exactly regular customers of divorce lawyers." he added. "And my services aren't exactly recommended for use as an alternate to going through the courts."

Takata narrowed his eyes as he studied the board. "Actually, I was hoping you could assist me with the courts." he said, not falling for the trap Dokuhotei had laid for him and moving a different piece.

Dokuhotei was surprised, Takata normally didn't show this level of tactical aptitude. "Assist you in what way?" he asked, contemplating the board.

Takata sat back and crossed his arms as he waited for Dokuhotei to make his next move. "I was hoping you could talk to my wifes lawyers and the judge on my behalf, make them see my point of view." he explained.

"I see." Dokuhotei said, running scenarios through his head. "That we would be more than capable of helping you with." he said, stroking his chin. "Though this is something that is more than just a favor, you do realize that?"

Takata nodded and fished around in his jacket pocket. 'I know." he said. 'My father in law is the President of Megatech Cybernetics Incorporated." he added, pulling out a USB key and sliding it over to him. "I'm sure he would be most grateful for keeping this quiet and out of the media."

'Really." Dokuhotei said as he caught the key. "Whats this?"

"Files." Takata said. "Of projects that Megatech is working on. Including full-body replacement."

Dokuhotei looked up in surprise at Takata. "You're joking." he asked. "The government is years away from approving cybernetic prosthesis."

Takata smiled. "Nope." he said. "I have a friend who has an inside line to MITI. They're fast tracking approval." he added putting his hands behind his head. "Within the next year and a half, it'll be approved."

Twirling the USB key in his fingers Dokuhotei gave Takata a shrewed look. "How?" he asked. "How did they manage that?"

'Easy." Takata answered. "They agreed to give the government all the bleeding edge stuff- hardware, software, etc."

Dokuhotei nodded, looking back at the board. Outside he was calm, but inside his mind was racing. There had been lots of rumors flying around about the government approving cybernetics, but the Hagakure didn't have any sources with access to the information that could prove it.

One of the rumors had said that Megatech was one of the companies on the shortlist to receive approval, and Takata had walked in and confirmed the rumors that they had been hearing -with the kicker giving the cal access to the head of the company.

Sighing, Dokuhotei looked up at Takata. "Checkmate." he said. "You win."

Takata chuckled and bowed to Dokuhotei. "Thank you." he said. "Do we have a deal?"

Dokuhotei smiled and nodded. "Oh, yes, Takata." he said. "We most certainly do."

Kenji looked up as Ai sat down next to him on the curb. She glanced up at him momentarily, then looked back down at her cone. He looked back at his own cone for a moment, feeling slightly uncomfortable.

Chewing on his lip for a few moments he looked back to his niece. "S- so." he stammered. "What did you get?"

Ai licked her lips and looked up at her uncle. "Mint..." she said quietly.

Kenji nodded. "I got mocha chino." he said. Ai nodded and they licked their cones for a few minutes before Kenji spoke again. "I heard you like to draw." he said.

Ai looked up at her uncle with a somewhat surprised look on her face. "Y-yeah." she said. "Daddy mentioned that you're a manga -ka."

"I am." Kenji said. "Matoko is my writer." he added, nodding where Matoko and Bre were fussing over Honzo.

Ai looked at Matoko for a few moments. "She's pretty." she commented.

"Thank you." Kenji said as Ai looked back to him.

"She's your girlfriend." Ai said, her tone stating that it was a fact.

"Yeah." Kenji replied. "You're very observant."

His niece looked down at her cone and took a lick of it. "Daddy says that a lot about me." she said. "He's very proud of it."

"He said you're the best thing that ever happened to him." Kenji said. "You and Honzo are the light of his life."

Ai nodded and licked her cone a couple of more times. "I think Daddy and Mommy are going to get a divorce." she said suddenly, making Kenji almost choke on his ice cream.

Coughing a couple of times, Kenji cleared his throat and looked at Ai. "How did you know that?" he said, not even trying to hide the question.

"They've been arguing a lot." Ai said. "And Mom's started going out a lot without dad."

Kenji blinked a couple of times and gave his niece a long look. "You don't sound too unhappy about your parents breaking up." he said cautiously.

Ai shrugged and licked her cone. "I think we're more of an inconvenience for Mom." she said. "I know Daddy would rather be with us."

Kenji chewed his lip for a few moments before nodding again. "Well..." he said "At least your father has his priorities right."

"Yeah." she said. "I hope we get to go with Daddy when it comes."

"I hope so too." Kenji said. "I'd like to get to know you and your brother better."

Ai 'hmmed' and went back to her cone. She and Kenji finished their cones and waited for her brother and the girls to finish up with theirs. As they waited Kenji nudged Ai with his arm.

"Hey." Kenji said. "You mind if I see your work?" he asked.

'I- I didn't bring anything with me..." stammered Ai, but Kenji held up his hand.

"I have plenty of paper." he said. "And pencils."

"Oh." Ai said. "I guess I wouldn't mind then."

Kenji nodded as Matoko came over with Honzo and Bre.

"Hey, Kenji." Bre said "Did you know Honzo here is a Quake fan?" she added.

Kenji and Ai turned to face Honzo. "Really?" he asked, getting a nod from both his nephew and Bre.

"Yeah." she said. "He says he's really good." Bre added. "In fact, he said he could beat He who Ownz." Honzo punctuated with a nod.

"Oh." Kenji said, a smile on his face. "Ai was going to show me how good of an artist she is, so I think we should head over to the apartment." he added.

"Good idea." Bre said, trading a knowing look with Kenji. Behind her, Matoko looked lost in thought -obviously the exchange had sparked her imagination and she was daydreaming.

Grabbing the now daydreaming Matoko, Bre followed Kenji as they went back the way they came and headed for the apartment.

The early afternoon sun lit the study with a golden glow as The Ninetails, Kyuubi Kawashima leaned back in his chair and pondered. Like all kitsunes, he was pondering mischief and mayhem, but it was all secondary to what was foremost on his mind: the Forbidden Scrolls of the Hagakure Clan.

He had gotten an email from Aki Dazi forty -five minutes earlier, reporting what he had overheard of Akihito and Asako's conversation. That his son had been unsuccessful in obtaining the scrolls, he had taken that for a given. The Hagakure were simply too good to allow anyone near the scrolls.

Sending his son into the clans territory had simply been to probe their defenses and get an idea of what he was up against. He just hadn't expected them to be that good, otherwise he would have sent a more powerful fox.

The reason Kyuubi had sent Akihito in to retrieve the scrolls hadn't been to increase his power- far from it. Kyuubi already knew all the techniques listed on the scrolls. The reason he had sent Akihito in to retrieve the scrolls had been to keep them from falling into the hands of a weaker demon and upsetting the balance of power.

Which, if what Aki had said in his email was correct, had just happened. Naota Sano, a young tai -yokai with the potential to become equal in power to Sheshy -poo and himself, had just gone from a minor unknown entity to a full blown threat to the status quo.

What worried Kyuubi about that wasn't how Sheshy -poo would react to this- he would react with his usual cold logic, resorting to violence only if Naota proved to be a threat to him or his lands. No, what worried Kyuubi was how all the other, lower powered demons would react.

There was a spiders web of alliances and favors all over the yokai community of Japan, and the slightest shift could cause a full blown war to ignite. And Kyuubi knew if that happened, it would be only a matter of time before the humans noticed, no matter how good a job the T.P.C.D did covering it up.

And when that happened, Kyuubi knew it would be all over. For no matter how powerful he and his fellow yokai may be, there was no way they could withstand over six billion humans.

Sighing, he began to drum his finger on his desk. There was no way he could prevent this from getting out indefinitely, for Naota would make his presence know once he started to increase in power. But, for now, he could keep it contained to give him some time while he pursued other options.

Yes, that would work.

Reaching over, he pressed the intercom on his desk. "Miss Kadiri." he said.

"Yeah boss?" his secretary answered over the intercom.

"When my son arrives, have everyone in the car come up to meet me." Kyuubi told her. "And tell them they are not to talk to anyone either."

"Sure thing boss." Kadiri said before Kyuubi broke the connection.
