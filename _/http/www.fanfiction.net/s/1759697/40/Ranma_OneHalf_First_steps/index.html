"And where were you?" Matoko asked.

"Naotas. I would have called, but his father slipped me something in a cup of tea." Lia answered as she dropped the blanket that had been wrapped around her.

Before Lia could say anything she found herself wrapped in a hug from Matoko.

"I was worried." Matoko said.

'I know." Lia answered. "And I'm sorry for doing that, but I was worried about Naota, and you know me, I gotta be the protector…"

"I know." Matoko said as she squeezed tighter. As she hugged her sister she became aware of something. "Lia, where's your bra?"

"erk!" was Lias response as her eyes went wide. Pulling away from her sister she looked up in Matokos face. 'If I tell you, promise you wont flip?" she asked.

Matoko nodded and then Lia continued.

"It's probably still in the Sanos dryer, along with my panties and the rest of my clothes." She said. "Kamon lent me these sweats of Naotas while I ran my clothes through the dryer."

'I was wondering who's they were." Matoko said as she stepped over to the closet where Lia kept her futon (3).

Pulling out the futon she laid it out while Lia pulled out her bedding. Unfolding the blanket Lia laid it out over her futon and then got into her futon. As she pulled the covers over herself she looked at her sister.

'Matoko, can you keep a secret?" Lia asked.

"Yes." Matoko answered with a nod.

"Well, come here. I don't want Christian to hear this." Lia said.

Nodding in understanding Matoko walked over to her sister and knelt down.

'What is it?" Matoko asked.

Leaning in to where her mouth was almost a centimeter from her sisters ear Lia spoke.

'Naota told me he loved me." She whispered.

Matokos eyes went as wide as dinner plates and she clamped her hands over her mouth to keep from squealing in delight. After several deep breaths she managed to get herself under control and carefully removed her hands from her mouth.

"He did?" she whispered.

At Lias nod she glomped her sister in a Saotome Sibling Glomp and tried to squeeze the life out of her.

'Congratulations." Matoko whispered in Lias ear.

'Thanks Matoko." Lia whispered back. "Now could you let go please? I can't breathe."

'Oh, I'm sorry." Matoko said as she released her sister. Looking at her she smiled. "So what are you going to do next?"

'I don't know." Lia answered. "And I don't think Naota does either."

Flopping back onto the futon Lia got comfortable. Looking up at her sister she flashed a smile.

"But we'll find out!" she said.

Matoko chuckled as she stood up.

'You know, there are times I envy you and Naota." She said. "At least you two can be candid with each other. Kenjis clueless."

"Don't lose heart Matoko." Lia said. "Kenjis shy, remember? It's taken a year for him to get so where he didn lock up when hee talks to you."

"I know." Matoko said as she looked at the floor. Playing with the carpet with her toe she continued. "And that's what makes him so cute."

Lia smiled as she curled up into a ball.

'Whatever you say, Matoko.' She said. "Wake me for dinner."

Matoko gave her sister a slight bow before turning and leaving. Left in the silence of her room Lia relaxed and was soon fast asleep, her mothers screaming from downstairs a comforting presence.

Naota hummed to himself as he leapt across rooftops to the Saotome home. He was heading over there after school to help Lia get over her cold and to get some studying done. He had had an easy day in class, surprisingly. The usual shenanigans of his classmates and the girls in school not affecting him at all.

Naota privately suspected that it was because in comparison of the events of the day before, it was nothing.

"There is something to be said about a life or death situation changing your priorities." Naota mused as he neared the Saotome house.

After landing silently in the yard Naota headed around to the front of the house and rang the doorbell. As he waited he undid the top buttons on his uniform jacket and fanned himself.

"Man, I hate it when we switch over to winter uniforms." He thought as footsteps sounded coming up to the door.

The latches in the door popped open and the door swung back, revealing Akane on the other side.

'Hello Naota." She said as she stepped back to allow Naota in.

"Sensei." Naota said as he stepped in.

Inside the mud room Naota shucked his uniform jacket and put it on his peg.

"Finally, I can be rid of that thing!" he said, fanning himself.

"They made the uniform switch early this year." Akane noted as she and Naota went inside the house.

Naota nodded. "Yeah. They changed it this year."

'Oh." Akane said as they entered the main room. "Well, anyways, Ranma made some miso soup for Lia. Why don't you run it up to her?"

'Yes sensei." Naota answered.

"And quit calling me that!" Akane said as she went to the kitchen. "We're not in the dojo and I'm not mad at you, just annoyed."

'Yes Akane." Naota answered.

'That's better." Akane said as she went into the kitchen. She stepped out a few moments later with a picture and a mug on a tray. "Here. Take this up." She said, handing it over to him.

Taking the tray Naota headed upstairs to Lias room. Pausing at her door he considered how he was going to open the door when Matoko came up the stairs behind him.

'Hey Matoko." Naota called out. "Could you give me a hand here?"

"Certainly Naota." She answered with a smile. Opening the door for him she flashed another smile at him before bowing to him." There you go."

'Thanks Matoko." Naota said as she walked down the hall to her room. Stepping into the room he saw Lia curled up on her futon. "Lunch." He called.

'Nyaow?" Lia asked sleepily as she uncurled. Pulling the covers back she looked at Naota. "Oh, hello Naota."

"Hello." Naota answered. "How are you feeling?"

Lia groaned as she sat up.

'Like I died and someone forgot to tell me." She said as she rubbed her eyes.

"Well, that's what you get for going out into the rain." Naota chided as he set the ray down on the floor next to Lia.

"And who's fault is that?" asked Lia.

'Touche'" Naota conceded with a nod as he sat down.

'So, how was school?" Lia asked as she picked up the mug.

"Oh, the usual." Naota said as Lia poured herself some miso soup. "Every girl there was chasing me, Suzuki was back to his old tricks-"

'I thought we pounded some sense into him." Lia interrupted.

"Apparently it didn't take." Naota said. Continuing on he began to pull homework from his bag. " And Pineapple brain tried to tag me because I was a minute late."

'What did you do?" Lia asked.

"Stuffed him into a locker with a couple of my old gym socks." Naota said with a shrug.

Lia winced and sipped her soup. Digging out a pencil Naota glanced up at Lia.

'Matoko was pretty happy." Naota commented.

"That's because I, ah, told her about our little conversation from yesterday." Lia said.

"I see." Naota answered. Looking at Lia he continued. "Because when I arrived your father didn't attack me I can take it you didn't tell your parents?" Naota asked.

"Yeah." Lia said with a nod. Sipping her soup she glanced at Naotas bag, which was bulging at the seams. "You picked up my homework for me?"

"I wish." Naota answered. "This is my make up homework and extra credit work I picked up so I can salvage my GPA."

'Oy." Lia said as she raised her eyebrows.

"Buckling down I see."

Lias eyes went wide and naota scowled at the sudden intrusion of the voice. Pointing a shaking finger lia tried to avoid spilling her soup as she spoke.

'Naota, there's a talking cat in my bedroom..." she said in a small voice.

"That's not a talking cat." Naota said as he turned his head to face the cat. 'Thats a kami."

"You remembered. I'm touched." the cat said with a smirk.

"Specifically it's the fuzzball that turned me into a demon." Naota said, a growl in his voice.

"It is?" Lia asked, staring at the cat. even for Nerima, a talking cat was out of the ordinary.

"I am." the cat said.

'So why are you here?" Naota growled as the cat began to groom it's paw. "You want to give me more problems or what?"

"No, I'm just here to check up on you and give you a heads up." the cat answered, totally unaffected by Naotas tone.

'What kind of 'heads up?" Naota asked.

'Well, for starters, you're going to have more to tell her parents than your declarations of love." the cat answered.

"What are you talking about? Tell my parents what?" Lia asked.

"That you two, by youkai standards, are already married." the cat said, the smirk evident in his voice. "That incident on the beach, what you did was mate."

Both were silent as they tried to comprehend what the kami had told them. Seeing their confusion the cat continued.

"You know what I'm talking about, don't you Naota?" the cat said. "Think about all the materials you've read from that priest at the shrine."

"The shrine?" Naota asked as he did a quick review of what he had learned. Slowly his jaw dropped as realization dawned on him. "Oh shit!" he hissed.

"What?" Lia asked as Naota gently took the cup of hot soup from her.

"He's right." Naota told her. "I read it in a text he e-mailed me."

"Why didn't you tell me?" Lia asked.

"Because I've been a little busy Lia, remember? Training, the clan?" Naota answered. " I read it before we went to the beach, but I was so busy I forgot all about it."

"Naota no baka." Lia answered, pulling a small mallet from Hammerspace and giving Naota a tap on the head.

"Tell me about it." Naota muttered. "So now what do we do?" he asked, looking at the cat.

The cat flicked its ears back and forth in a feline version of a shrug.

"I'm not going to tell you what your next course of action should be." the cat said as it turned around to face the door. Looking back at them it continued. "You two are doing well enough on your own. And on that note I'm bidding you both adieu."

"Wait-" Naota said as the cat walked through the door and was gone. "damn."

Naota sighed and handed Lia back her soup.

"Well, this is completely out of left field." he said as Lia nodded in agreement.

"Two questions though. One, how exactly are we going to tell our parents about this?" Lia asked.

Naota rubbed his eyes and looked up at Lia.

"We'll think of something." he said. "And the other?"

"When I get cold can I snuggle up against you? You're nice and warm, plus you make a dandy pillow."

'Won't your parents object?" Naota asked.

"No." Lia answered, shaking her head. 'I do that everybody."

'Oh. Okay." naota answered, not sure of what he'd gotten himself into.
